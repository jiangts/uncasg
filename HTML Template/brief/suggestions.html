A New News Model

To create a profitable business model for news media corporations, we propose combining a future-ready digital model with the classical print model.

The digital model will consist of a website and a mobile app. Consumers can subscribe to a paid, premium version of the digital model, which allows them to view an unlimited number of articles per month, as opposed to a cap on monthly article views in the free version of the digital model. In addition, the free digital access will include interstitial advertisements to provide another revenue line for news media corporations.

To integrate this with the classical print model, we propose that along with a daily subscription to the print newspaper, a consumer will receive access to the premium version of the digital model. This will give consumers an incentive to purchase the print media, further expanding the traditional revenue stream.

The New York Times has proven the solidity of this print-digital model. Within a year of introducing a similar premium content subscription, they gained 454,000 subscribers. The premium content, in addition to advertising, will fortify the revenue from the print media subscription, allowing news media corporations to continue to provide high quality journalism.

In addition, the increased revenue stream can go towards hiring journalists on a contract basis (similar to a professor’s tenure). This allows journalists to focus on a higher quality of journalism, rather than worrying about deadlines or possible controversy, by ensuring job security. This will result in an overall increase in the quality of news reported, and may lead to a long-term increase in subscribers.

This model is better than the current accepted model of journalism for two main reasons: it provides incentive for more consumers to subscribe, and provides more sources of revenue for news media companies. With the increase in demand for “news right now,” having a news source that can provide that is imperative to maintain the bottom line. The website and mobile app provide that, adding another line of revenue, while fortifying that existing revenue source (print media). Revenue is not just obtained from subscriptions though. Ads will also be displayed on the “free” version of the mobile app and website. Ads make up a major part of both digital and print news media corporation’s profits (65% in 2012), so this advertising in the digital model will provide yet another revenue stream. This revenue allows our model, as a whole, to maximize profits, while maintaining the need for a 'free and open' internet.
